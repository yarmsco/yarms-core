↻ The loop

This is the part that makes it "iterate on itself" — a human approves every change; nothing auto-swaps.

1

Clients run on the Registry's model & emit usage + eval samples.

2

Control plane reads the telemetry, runs an A/B on cheaper models.

3

Posts a ranked proposal to Slack — savings + confidence.

4

You approve (or not) in Slack.

5

On yes, it writes model_registry.

6

Every client picks up the new model on its next call — no redeploy.

→ new telemetry flows in → back to step 1 · the clients and the rules improve each other
What moves, to get here: the running tools currently inside yarms_agents/internal_tools/ — the audit_agent and the usage report — move out into the private yarms-control-plane (which imports yarms-core), joined by the new efficiency + build-report loops. yarms_agents loses internal_tools/ and becomes just another client. Secrets never get committed anywhere — public or private — they always live in the runtime.
